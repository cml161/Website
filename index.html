About

Researcher.
Builder.
Leader.

I'm a 4th-year Biomedical Engineering PhD Candidate at Case Western Reserve University, working in Dominique Durand's Lab, focused on suppressing mesial temporal lobe epilepsy through innovative neuroengineering approaches. Leading a team of undergraduate researchers, I develop novel passive decoupling and electric-field modulation strategies to interrupt seizure generation and propagation.

Before my PhD I spent time at Abbott Laboratories in bioformulation R&D, and earned my B.S. in Biological Engineering at The Ohio State University.

Outside the lab, I co-founded CWRU Lift to build a community that shares my passion for health and fitness, yielding over 300 students and securing over $10,000 in sponsorships and local business partnerships. I believe great science doesn't stay in the lab.

Fun fact: I'm multilingual, studied organic chemistry in South Korea, and have a deep and growing passion for music production. Sound engineering has a surprisingly grand overlap with neural engineering.

Patent Pending · Neural Implant

Fara Passive Seizure Suppression Device

Fara is a patent-pending passive neural implant designed to suppress ephaptic (electric-field coupled) seizure propagation in the hippocampus — requiring no external power, no stimulation, and no active components. A perforated conductive film that simply interrupts the field.

Developed at the Durand Lab, Case Western Reserve University. Currently advancing through preliminary clinical evaluation stages.
